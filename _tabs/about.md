My name is Tyler Visentin. I'm a U.S. Army veteran (Airborne Infantry) currently transitioning into software engineering. After leaving the military, I was drawn to the creative and fulfilling process of building software—crafting solutions from scratch, solving intricate problems, and witnessing ideas come to life. While my background in cybersecurity has instilled in me a strong appreciation for secure development, I'm excited to pursue a degree in software engineering to deepen my expertise in this dynamic field. I'm still early in my journey, but I'm fully committed to growing every day and honing my skills in development.

I've started working with tools like Kali Linux, Nmap, and Burp Suite, spending time learning how systems work by actually using them—testing, breaking, and figuring things out. I've also experimented with running AI locally and understanding how different systems interact. I don't claim to know everything—I'm here to learn, build, and improve.

I created this blog to document my journey and hold myself accountable. Instead of just saying I'm learning software engineering, I want to show it through real projects, notes, and research. This space will be where I share what I'm working on, what I'm learning, and how I'm progressing over time.

Right now, my focus is on building practical skills in areas like software development, system architecture, and integrating best practices for robust and secure applications. I also plan to work on projects like developing web applications, experimenting with emerging technologies, and continuing to expand my knowledge through hands-on experience.

The goal is simple: become highly skilled in software engineering, and build a body of work that demonstrates my capabilities. This blog is part of that process.
