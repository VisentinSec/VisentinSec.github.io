My name is Tyler Visentin. I'm a U.S. Army veteran (Airborne Infantry) currently transitioning into cybersecurity. After leaving the military, I was looking for something that still gave me that same sense of challenge, purpose, and constant growth—and I found that in cybersecurity. I'm still early in the process, but I'm fully committed to getting better every day and building real, hands-on skills.

I've started working with tools like Kali Linux, Nmap, and Burp Suite, spending time learning how systems work by actually using them—testing, breaking, and figuring things out. I've also experimented with running AI locally and understanding how different systems interact. I don't claim to know everything—I'm here to learn, build, and improve.

I created this blog to document my journey and hold myself accountable. Instead of just saying I'm learning cybersecurity, I want to show it through real projects, notes, and research. This space will be where I share what I'm working on, what I'm learning, and how I'm progressing over time.

Right now, my focus is on building practical skills in areas like reconnaissance, web application testing, vulnerability research, and offensive security. I also plan to work on projects like building a honeypot, experimenting with security tools, and continuing to expand my knowledge through hands-on experience.

The goal is simple: become highly skilled in cybersecurity, especially in offensive security and red teaming, and build a body of work that proves it. This blog is part of that process.
