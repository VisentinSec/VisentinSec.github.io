_Pool Liners — premium lead-gen website for a family-owned Tennessee pool company_

## What I Built

Pool Liners is a clean, premium React website for a family-owned pool liner company based in Tennessee. The goal was a site that looks high-end and instantly communicates quality and trust — something that could compete with larger regional competitors.

## The Problem

Small pool businesses often have outdated websites (or no website at all) that don't reflect the quality of their actual work. A new liner installation is a $2,000–$5,000 decision for a homeowner. The website needs to justify that spend before a customer ever picks up the phone.

## Features

- **Product Showcase** — liner patterns, styles, and material options with photo galleries
- **Service Overview** — installation process explained step-by-step (homeowners want to know what to expect)
- **Service Area** — Tennessee counties and surrounding regions
- **Quote Request Flow** — captures pool size, current liner condition, and timeline
- **Before/After Gallery** — the most persuasive content for this type of business
- **FAQ Section** — answers the questions every homeowner asks before calling

## Tech Stack

- React 18 + TypeScript
- Vite
- Tailwind CSS
- Fully static

## Design Decisions

Pool liner replacement has a clear seasonal urgency (people want their pools ready for summer). The design leans into that — the hero section is bold, the CTAs are prominent, and there's a clear path from "I found this site" to "I submitted a quote request" in under 60 seconds.

I also paid close attention to mobile performance. Pool owners searching "pool liner replacement near me" are often on their phones in the backyard looking at a damaged liner. The site loads fast and the phone number is one tap away on every page.
